# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is one person checking a personal Android phone throughout the
day. They need to confirm a once-daily medication task quickly, often from a
notification or with a brief glance at the home screen. The product must remain
usable when the user is rushed, distracted, offline, or looking at the phone in
low light.

## Product Purpose

The app answers one question: whether today's medication has been taken. It
provides a single daily record, a configurable local reminder, persistent repeat
notifications until the task is resolved, editable history, and a compact monthly
summary. Success means the user can understand today's state and update it in one
tap without managing medicines, accounts, schedules, or cloud data.

## Brand Personality

Calm, direct, private. The interface should feel like a dependable personal
utility rather than a healthcare portal, habit game, or motivational product.
Copy is short and factual. Feedback is clear without celebration, judgment, or
pressure.

## Anti-references

- Do not resemble a hospital system, pharmacy dashboard, or cold blue medical UI.
- Do not use streaks, badges, confetti, guilt, rankings, or gamified adherence.
- Do not expose medication-related language in the launcher name, app icon, or
  system notifications.
- Do not bury the daily confirmation behind navigation, forms, or multiple cards.
- Do not add medicine catalogs, multiple dose times, accounts, charts, or cloud
  features to the first version.

## Design Principles

1. One glance, one action: today's state and primary action are always first.
2. Privacy outside, clarity inside: external surfaces stay generic while the open
   app uses explicit medication language.
3. State before decoration: pending, taken, and missed must be distinguishable by
   text and icon as well as color.
4. Notifications obey the record: completion cancels reminders immediately, and
   reminder controls never silently rewrite history.
5. Local and dependable: core recording works without permissions, connectivity,
   accounts, or a server.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA contrast where applicable. Touch targets are at least 44 by 44
CSS pixels. Status is never communicated by color alone. The app follows the
system light or dark preference, supports reduced motion, uses plain Chinese copy,
and avoids time-limited interactions. Text remains readable at larger Android font
scales without overlap or truncation.
